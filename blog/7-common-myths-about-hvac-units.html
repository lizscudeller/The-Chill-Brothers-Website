7 Common Myths About HVAC Units

There are numerous myths and false beliefs out there concerning how heating and cooling systems work. Sometimes it can be hard to differentiate truths from myths, especially when you think you are saving money on bills. While many people don’t know how their HVAC systems work, knowing how your unit works is the best way to differentiate between the truth and the myths. Below are seven myths about HVAC systems that most people believe:

  1. HVAC unit maintenance is a waste of money
    Regular maintenance of your unit is essential for it to function correctly. Most people go to the dentist for a checkup to make sure the teeth are healthy. The same aspect applies to your HVAC system. Your unit consists of many parts, such as motors, coils, and belts that work together to warm and cool your house. Regular maintenance checks ensure that every part is working efficiently. If you don’t schedule regular maintenance with your HVAC contractor, your unit could malfunction and lead to unnecessary and expensive repairs in the middle of winter. Regular maintenance also ensures that your system is energy efficient.
  2. Changing your air filters once every year is enough
    Air filters help improve your indoor air quality. Throughout the year, dust particles, debris, and other indoor contaminants can build up in your air filters. If you decide to replace the filter after one year, it will be extremely dirty and clogged. A clogged air filter blocks airflow making the unit work harder to circulate the air into your home. Air filters should be replaced every few months and cleaned regularly to ensure that the unit is functioning efficiently and clean air is being circulated in the house.
  3. Closing my unit’s vents or registers will reduce energy consumption
    Even though the registers and vents have levers for closing off airflow, it’s not a good idea to close them. No matter how many vents or registers are closed, your HVAC unit will output the same amount of cooled air. Blocking the vents will affect the system’s air intake and output. This could also lead to pressure and airflow problems.
  4. Turning the HVAC unit on and off will conserve energy
    Your HVAC system doesn’t work like your lights and other household appliances. Turning your lights off and on when you don’t need them can save you energy. However, doing the same with the HVAC unit will lead to higher energy consumption. The best way to save on energy consumption is by installing a programmable thermostat and turning it to lower temperature settings when you’re away from home. This way, you will be reducing its energy consumption.
  5. The bigger the unit, the better it will work
    If you like a bigger unit, you can get one. However, a bigger unit doesn’t always mean it will work better. Before buying yout HVAC system, consult with a specialist to explore the best option for you, depending on your household’s needs and your square footage. If you purchase an oversized unit, your home may end up being damp. It’s essential to find the right HVAC unit size for your house.
  6. You should cover your cooling system during the cold months
    Your AC was designed to withstand outdoor weather conditions such as temperature fluctuations, snow, and rain. Covering it could lead to problems. Crawl animals such as rodents might turn your AC unit into a home when you cover it. This could lead to numerous issues such as chewed wires and other damages. Covering your AC system can also trap moisture and warmth, creating the perfect environment for mold and mildew to grow. You don’t need to cover your unit during winter.
  7. The fan will cool down my house
    The fan doesn’t function the same as the Air Conditioner. A fan is only designed to circulate air in a room, not cool it down. While you may feel like the fan is cooling you down by passing air across the skin, it can’t lower the room temperature.

If you have any questions about the best HVAC practices, seek assistance from an expert. Don’t just believe everything you read on the internet or what other people tell you. While your HVAC unit is a complex system and understanding exactly how it works can be challenging, filtering out myths and misconceptions can help you save on energy consumption and ensure that the system is functioning efficiently.
